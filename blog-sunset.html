Sunset

Psalm 65:8 "They also that dwell in the uttermost parts are afraid at thy tokens: thou makest the outgoings of the morning and evening to rejoice."

Some people describe a sunset as a dying day. It certainly appeals to our dramatic side and has something of a majestic flair when compared with the mythical phoenix. But in the end, they are still comparing one of the most beautiful sights in this world to death, a word we associate with gloomy colors and sadness. Yes, there is death in a sunset, but there is also hope and joy. There's hope of the day that will follow the coming night, and joy in that hope.

When I look at a sunset, I don't see just the death of a day. I see the sun going out in a blaze of glory; dying, yes, but reminding us that it will return again. As the sun sinks beneath the horizon to where we can no longer see it, it paints the sky with its most beautiful and vibrant colors, somehow richer and deeper than those of the sunrise, to remind us that it will return. Even the darkest night, even the cloudiest day, cannot keep the sun's light from us forever. Sooner or later, it will return and we will see it once again.

In this, I see an allegory of Christ. Luke 23:44-46 "And it was about the sixth hour, and there was a darkness over all the earth until the ninth hour. And the sun was darkened, and the veil of the temple was rent in the midst. And when Jesus had cried with a loud voice, he said, Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit: and having said thus, he gave up the ghost." When He gave His life for us, the whole sky darkened prematurely. It became as night while the day had several hours left, as if to signify that the Light of this world had passed from it for a time. And yet neither Death nor Hell could hold Him any longer than the three days He had said He would be gone for. Mark 8:31"And he began to teach them, that the Son of man must suffer many things, and be rejected of the elders, and of the chief priests, and scribes, and be killed, and after three days rise again." Sure enough, Mary and some of the other women who had followed Jesus went to His tomb as the sun was rising on the third day, only to find that the Son of God had already risen from the dead. Mark 16:2-6 "And very early in the morning the first day of the week, they came unto the sepulchre at the rising of the sun... And when they looked, they saw that the stone was rolled away: for it was very great. And entering into the sepulchre, they saw a young man sitting on the right side, clothed in a long white garment; and they were affrighted. And he saith unto them, Be not affrighted: Ye seek Jesus of Nazareth, which was crucified: he is risen; he is not here: behold the place where they laid him." The Light had returned to the world to lead us out of darkness and into the eternal light of Heaven and fellowship with the Father!

Finally, I see a hope for our own deaths. When we lose a loved one, it seems our whole world grows darker for a time. And yet, as in the brilliant colors of the setting sun we find hope that it will rise again, so we find hope that we, too, will rise again as the Word says. 1 Corinthians 15:51-54 "Behold, I shew you a mystery; We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed, In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed. For this corruptible must put on incorruption, and this mortal must put on immortality. So when this corruptible shall have put on incorruption, and this mortal shall have put on immortality, then shall be brought to pass the saying that is written, Death is swallowed up in victory." Death may hold us longer than it did our Saviour, but he will have to relinquish his hold on us someday and we will be reunited with our loved ones who are saved. Then we will see God and live in the light of His glory forever! There will be no sun and no sunsets in Heaven because there will be no need for them. We will no longer need to be reminded that darkness does not last forever or that death is not permanent because there is no death or darkness in Heaven, no sadness or sin, no fear, no pain, and no suffering.

This is the message of hope I see.

So what do you see when you look at a sunet? Do you see a herald of the coming night or a promise of the coming light?
